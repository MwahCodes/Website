    • WHY YOU MIGHT ASK?

    On March 2003, U.S. forces invaded Iraq vowing to destroy
    weapons of mass destruction (WMD) and end the dictatorial
    rule of Saddam Hussein. When WMD intelligence proved illusory and a
    violent insurgency arose, the war lost public support. Saddam was
    captured, tried, and hanged and democratic elections were held.
    In the years since, there have been over 4,700 U.S. and allied
    troop deaths, and more than one hundred thousand Iraqi civilians
    have been killed. Meanwhile, questions linger over Iraq’s fractious
    political situation.
